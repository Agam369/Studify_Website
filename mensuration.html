Mensuration

Mensuration is the branch of mathematics that studies the measurement of geometric figures and their parameters like length, volume, shape, surface area, lateral surface area, etc. In our earlier classes, we have learnt to find the perimeter and area of various plane figures like rectangle, square, triangle, parallelogram and rhombus. We have also learnt to find areas of pathways. Now,we will learn to calculate the areas of some more plane figures. We will also learn to find the surface area and volume of some solids, such as cuboid, cube and cylinder.



The terms related to Mensuration are:-

Perimeter
- It is the total distance around the shape or the length of the boundary of any closed shape.
Area
- Area is the amount of space occupied by a two-dimensional figure. It is expressed in square units.
Surface Area
- Surface Area is the total area occupied by the surfaces of a 3D object. They are classified into two - Curved or Lateral Surface Area and Total Surface Area.
Volume
- It is the amount of space occupied by a 3D shape. It is expressed in cubic units.
Dimensions
- they are the measurement of the length, width or height of some object.


Uses of Mensuration

Mensuration is an important topic with high applicability in real-life scenarios. Given below are some of the scenarios.

  1. Measurement of agricultural fields, floor areas required for purchase/selling transactions.
  2. Measurement of volumes required for packaging milk, liquids, solid edible food items.
  3. Measurements of surface areas required for estimation of painting houses, buildings, etc.
  4. Volumes and heights are useful in knowing water levels and amounts in rivers or lakes.
  5. Optimum cost packaging sachets for milk etc. like tetra packing.



3D & 2D figures

The figures which have only length and breadth are called 2-dimensional figures such as circle, square, rectange, triangle etc. And the figures which have all the 3 dimensions length, breadth and height are called 3-dimensional figures such as cone, sphere, cuboic, cube, cylinder etc.

Example

Question: The area of a trapezium shaped field is 480 m2, the distance between two parallel sides is 15 m and one of the parallel sides is 20 m. Find the other parallel side.

Solution:
One of the parallel sides of the trapezium is a = 20 m, let another parallel side be b, height h = 15 m.
The given area of trapezium = 480 m2
We know, by formula;
Area of a trapezium = ½ h (a+b)
480 = ½ (15) (20+b)
20 + b = (480×2)/15
b = 64 – 20 = 44 m
Total Surface Area of Solid Shapes
TSA of Cuboid = 2(lb + bh + hl)
TSA of Cube = 6l2
TSA of Cylinder = 2πr (r + h)
